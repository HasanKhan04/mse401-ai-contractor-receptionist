# Requirements Analysis

## Requirements Derivation

The requirements were derived from the problem analysis, stakeholder needs, and competitor analysis. The team identified the primary tasks currently performed by receptionists in small home service businesses and translated these responsibilities into functional requirements. Non-functional requirements were established to ensure the system provides a reliable and effective customer experience.

## Functional Requirements

| ID  | Requirement                       | Priority | Acceptance Criteria                | Rationale                                                                        |
| --- | --------------------------------- | -------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| FR1 | Answer incoming calls             | High     | Call answered within 3 seconds     | Reduces missed customer inquiries and improves accessibility                     |
| FR2 | Collect customer information      | High     | Name and phone number recorded     | Enables lead capture and follow-up communication                                 |
| FR3 | Identify customer service request | High     | Service request recorded correctly | Allows businesses to understand customer needs and schedule appropriate services |
| FR4 | Schedule appointments             | High     | Appointment added to calendar      | Automates one of the primary responsibilities of a receptionist                  |
| FR5 | Answer frequently asked questions | High     | Accurate response provided         | Reduces repetitive inquiries and improves customer experience                    |

## Non-Functional Requirements

| ID   | Requirement  | Acceptance Criteria                                        | Rationale                                                                 |
| ---- | ------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| NFR1 | Availability | 24/7 operation                                             | Customers may call outside normal business hours                          |
| NFR2 | Reliability  | 99% uptime                                                 | Ensures the system remains available for customer interactions            |
| NFR3 | Scalability  | Support multiple calls per day without service degradation | Allows the system to accommodate business growth and varying call volumes |
| NFR4 | Performance  | Response time under 3 seconds                              | Maintains a natural and responsive customer experience                    |

## Requirement Prioritization

All functional requirements have been assigned a High priority because they represent the core responsibilities of the proposed AI receptionist. The non-functional requirements were selected to ensure the system remains accessible, reliable, scalable, and responsive while supporting the needs of small home service businesses.
