# Problem Analysis

Small home service businesses such as plumbers, electricians, HVAC technicians, landscapers, and general contractors frequently face challenges managing customer communications. Unlike larger organizations with dedicated office staff, many small businesses operate with limited administrative resources. Owners and technicians often spend most of their time traveling between job sites, performing services, and meeting customers, leaving little opportunity to answer incoming phone calls consistently.

For many home service businesses, phone calls represent the primary method through which customers request services, schedule appointments, and obtain information. When calls are missed, customers may choose to contact competing businesses rather than leave a voicemail or wait for a callback. Industry research suggests that missed calls can lead to lost revenue, reduced customer satisfaction, and missed business opportunities. As customer expectations continue to increase, businesses are expected to provide faster response times and more accessible communication channels.

Many small businesses currently rely on voicemail systems when staff are unavailable. While voicemail is inexpensive and easy to implement, it does not provide immediate assistance to customers. Customers are often required to leave a message and wait for a response, which can create frustration and increase the likelihood of contacting a competitor. Hiring a dedicated receptionist can improve customer service but may not be financially feasible for many small businesses due to labour costs and limited operating hours.

Recent advances in artificial intelligence have led to the development of AI-powered voice receptionists capable of answering calls, responding to customer inquiries, and automating routine business interactions. Industry reports and technology providers have highlighted the growing adoption of AI receptionists as businesses seek to improve customer service while reducing administrative workload. However, most existing solutions are designed as general-purpose platforms and require significant customization before they can effectively support the workflows of contractor and home service businesses.

The primary stakeholders affected by this problem include business owners, field technicians, office staff, and customers. Business owners are affected through lost leads, reduced operational efficiency, and increased administrative burden. Technicians may be interrupted during jobs to answer calls or return messages. Customers are affected through delayed responses, difficulties booking appointments, and inconsistent communication experiences.

Based on the problem analysis and review of existing solutions, the project team identified an opportunity to develop a contractor-focused AI receptionist specifically designed for small home service businesses. The proposed system will answer incoming calls, collect customer information, identify service requests, answer frequently asked questions, and schedule appointments automatically. By automating routine customer interactions while remaining available 24 hours a day, the system aims to improve customer accessibility, reduce missed business opportunities, and decrease the administrative workload placed on small business owners and staff.
